# Universe Definition (v0)

The initial artifact uses a static historical dataset derived from prior research infrastructure. The data is not live, not predictive, and is used solely to demonstrate sensitivity of portfolio construction to estimation noise and exogenous perturbations. Results are qualitative and methodological rather than commercial or allocative.

## Objective
Define a fixed, simple equity universe to study portfolio sensitivity under estimation noise and exogenous perturbations.

## Asset Selection Criteria
- Large-cap U.S. equities
- High liquidity
- Diverse economic exposures
- Widely understood by practitioners

## Included Assets
AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, META,
JPM, CAT, XOM,
JNJ, PG, KO,
AMT

## Exclusions
- ETFs
- Small-cap equities
- International equities
- Alternative assets
- Dynamic universe selection

## Rationale
The universe is intentionally static and non-optimized. Any observed instability arises from model sensitivity rather than asset selection.
